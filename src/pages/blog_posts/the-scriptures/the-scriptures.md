## Jerome
![Scholar writing scriptures][jerome]
St. Jerome was a Latin scholar born in Italy in the year 342 AD. Though skeptical of Christianity at first, he was eventually converted.
Jerome was very interested in Latin literature, and was a particular fan of the Roman author Cicero. During a journey through Asia Minor
and Syria in 373, Jerome fell very ill with a strong fever. In a letter that I will summarize here, he describes the experience he had that
changed the course of his life. *See the full text in [Latin][jerome-latin] or [English][jerome-english].*

While unable to sleep because of the suffering of the fever, Jerome says that he was whisked away by the Spirit and carried before the
judgement seat of Christ. When Christ asked him who he was, he responded "I am a Christian." Christ responded,

> **Thou liest**, thou art a follower of Cicero and not of Christ. For ‘where thy treasure is, there will thy heart be also.’

Jerome said that he suffered much torment and guilt in his vision. Upon awaking he changed the course of his life, and subsequently the course of the Christian world. Laying aside his studies
of worldly books, he dedicated himself to retranslating the Gospels of the New Testament from Greek to Latin. His project expanded, and
he ended up learning Hebrew and translating the Old Testament into Latin as well. His new translation of the Bible, The Vulgate Bible, came
to be the official version used by the Catholic church. His work opened the door so that many more people would have access to the Bible,
and paved the way for the modern translations we have today. Without Jerome's sacrifices and his focus on the scriptures, our religious
landscape would be entirely different. Jerome's love for the scriptures changed the world.
